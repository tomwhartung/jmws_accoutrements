About Affiliate Marketing

The internet gives us an opportunity to share our opinions about products we like and recommend them to visitors. To make it easy for visitors to obtain these products, we partner with certain merchants, known as affiliates, and use the affiliate marketing links they provide in our content.

When you click on one of these affiliate marketing links, the following happens:

  1. Your browser accesses the merchant's site — most likely in a new window or browser tab
  2. If your browser is configured to accept cookies, the merchant's site places a referral cookie in your browser
  3. If you make a purchase from the merchant before the cookie expires, the merchant pays us a commission for the referral
  4. These commissions help us maintain and improve this and our other sites

This type of reimbursement for our work in sharing our recommendations is known as affiliate marketing, and it helps us recoup some of the investment we have made in creating, providing, and maintaining this website.

A Win-Win-Win Situation

Note that following an affiliate marketing link then making a purchase does not add anything to the price you pay for the product.

Affiliate marketing is a type of performance-based marketing that offers a win-win-win situation for everyone involved:

  • Following the link and accepting the cookie costs you absolutely nothing
  • Merchants pay for the referral only if and when they make a sale
  • The commissions we earn this way help us continue to provide the site and develop new content for it

In other words, this is like free money — in a manner of speaking ☺.
